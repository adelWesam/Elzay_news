HR News

Dear Finance Team

We request to deduct an amount of 5,000 Jordanian dinars from non-Jordanian workers in the vests department who attended work today 6/1/25 due to their participation in an unauthorized strike.

The Human Resources Department will provide you with a list of the names of the workers in the vests department who attended work today.

Justification:

Referring to the Jordanian Labor Law:

Article 135 - Strike and Lockout Notification

The worker may not strike without notifying the employer at least fourteen days before the date set for the strike. This period is doubled if the work is related to one of the public interest services.

Article 136 - Prohibited Strike and Lockout

A. If any worker goes on a strike prohibited by this law, he shall be punished with a fine of not less than fifty Jordanian dinars for the first day and five dinars for each day the strike continues. He shall also lose his wages for the days he is on strike.

The workers in the jackets department went on an unauthorized strike on January 6, 25, in violation of these legal provisions.

Timeline of Events:

When the workers’ union contacted me earlier, I informed them that Jan’s salary would be paid on time, i.e., January 7, 25, if all procedures were completed.

I also clarified that, in any case, the maximum delay would be until Thursday, January 9, 25. This information was shared at their request to ensure they could notify their families in advance if there were any delays.

However, today, I received a report that employees in the Jacket Section had staged a strike after the break. I promptly met with the group and reiterated that salaries would be paid tomorrow, on time. As per company policy, a deduction of 5,000 JOD will be applied for today’s unauthorized strike action.

Additionally, employees from the Hugo Boss Section engaged in a brief strike. After I clarified that the same deduction policy would apply, they resumed their duties and confirmed they were not striking.

This update is being communicated promptly to ensure everyone is informed of the situation and the company's commitment to adhering to agreed timelines while addressing unauthorized absences in line with company policy.
